Attendance Allowance

Attendance Allowance is a tax-free, non-means-tested benefit for people over State Pension age who need frequent help or supervision with personal care due to a long-term illness or disability.

You must be State Pension age or older and have a condition (physical or mental) that means you need help with personal care tasks or supervision to stay safe, day or night. The condition must have lasted (or be expected to last) at least 6 months.

Lower rate: £72.65 per week (around £3,778 per year).
Higher rate: £108.55 per week (around £5,645 per year).

Pension Credit

Pension Credit is a means-tested benefit that tops up the weekly income of people over State Pension age. It can also unlock additional support like Housing Benefit help and Winter Fuel Payments.

You must be State Pension age or older and have a weekly income below the guarantee level (approximately £218 for singles or £333 for couples in 2025/26). Savings and capital affect the amount.

The average award is around £3,900 per year, though it varies based on individual circumstances.

Yes – usually up to 3 months from the date of application.

It can unlock additional support such as Council Tax reduction, Winter Fuel Payment, and a free TV licence if over 75.

Blue Badge

A Blue Badge is a parking permit that allows closer parking (e.g., on yellow lines, in disabled bays) for people with severe mobility problems, whether driving or as a passenger.

Automatic eligibility if you receive higher-rate mobility PIP/DLA or certain war pensions. Otherwise, if you have severe difficulty walking, are at risk when walking outdoors, or have certain conditions (assessed by your local council).

Applications are made through your local council. We provide full support, including completing the form and gathering evidence.

Up to £10 in England (free in Scotland and Wales). Valid for 3 years.

Councils aim to process within 12 weeks, though times vary by authority.
